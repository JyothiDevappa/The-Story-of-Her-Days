Better Together💛

Every trio has its rhythm —
One talks a lot, one listens deeply, and the third just vibes.

For me, it all started in the last four months of college.
A short time? Yes.
But what we lived in those days — that felt like a lifetime.

Before them, I never had a friendship like that. People heard me before, sure — but no one actually listened. But these two? Oh, they heard everything — even my overthinking.

Let me talk about my two girls.

One queen has curly hair — she’s an extrovert. She had a scooty, a laugh louder than her ringtone. She always wore kajal in her eyes — like she was giving an advertisement for it every day. 😄

She lived near our hostel. No matter what her mother said, if I was feeling low — especially during that never-ending hostel warden drama — she’d still show up.

She wasn’t going to solve my problems, but just riding around with her — wind in my face and laughter in my ears — was enough to forget everything.

She always matched my energy — movies, songs, dance... everything.

Every morning, she’d come to pick me up for college. I was always late, but she never complained. she always captured my moments on Snapchat — like it was our own little series.

The other one? Total opposite.

She had long, silky hair. Quiet. Introverted. She wouldn’t even go to the hotel alone. She seemed like someone who’d never talk to anyone — but with me? She did. Her nails were always sharp, and she was always rocking some new, stylish dress. Total modern girl vibes — not saree-type, but definitely a girly vibe. 😄

She loved me the most,
Every day after college, we were like foodies on a mission — eating golgappas outside.

I always used to tell her,
“Talk more, go outside, don’t be so shy!”

And now?? She’s out there talking to everyone, eating alone, walking like Miss Independent. I’m like — hellooo madam, slow down! You weren’t even going to the hotel alone back then, and now you’re out here like a heroine in her solo movie 😭.

three of us together? Different energy altogether.

There was the stone.
Yes — a stone.
Not a bench, not a fancy table. Just a big, wide stone under a huge tree in our college campus. That was our place.

Every break, every lunch, every stolen hour — we sat there. Played songs, talked about everything and nothing, clicked silly pictures, and shared whatever food someone had in their bag.

Before them? I sat on that stone alone. It felt like a dead place — just cold and empty. I was lonely, like background music in a sad movie.

But with them, that same spot became our happy place — full of laughter, music, and nonstop nonsense.

"I still remember that day — the three of us went for a ride, laughing like there was no tomorrow. We stopped at a hotel, they ordered set dosa, not because they liked it, but just because I did. That moment showed me how much they cared — choosing my favourite over theirs, just to see me smile.

Real friendship feels like that — warm, simple, and full of love." 😊✨
